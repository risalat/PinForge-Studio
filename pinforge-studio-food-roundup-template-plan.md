# PinForge Studio Food Roundup Template Plan

## Goal
Design a focused set of bold, food-first Pinterest templates for roundup articles such as:

- `27 Crockpot Soup Recipes`
- `25 Christmas Dessert Ideas`
- `31 Easy Summer Salads`
- `18 High Protein Breakfast Recipes`

This set should avoid drifting back into the recent scrapbook / editorial-paper / bookmark-ribbon family. The new set should feel:

- more food-native
- more appetite-driven
- more thumb-stopping at feed size
- more compatible with bold, high-contrast presets

## Design Principles For This Set

### Input constraint
These templates must assume PinForge Studio receives only finished dish / plated recipe images from roundup posts.

That means:
- no ingredient-only image requirements
- no menu-image requirements
- no recipe-card image requirements
- no sauce / texture asset requirements
- no extra decorative photos outside the article dish imagery

The layout itself can still reference food-adjacent aesthetics like menu boards, bakery signs, diner graphics, or recipe cards, but the actual image slots must all work with complete finished dish photos.

### Food-specific visual priorities
- Make the food imagery do more of the selling than the typography.
- Prefer layouts that show texture, garnish, steam, layers, glaze, crumbs, drips, frosting, cheese pull, soup surface, or plated variety.
- Keep titles highly legible at small feed sizes, especially for recipe keywords like `Soup`, `Dessert`, `Chicken`, `Breakfast`, `Salad`.
- Numbers should feel useful and graphic, not decorative clutter.

### What to avoid
- No more soft scrapbook / tape-board / paper-craft layouts for this set.
- No more “home decor” editorial language in the visual treatment.
- No subtle cream-on-cream systems that depend on refined taste. Food needs more immediate read.
- Avoid layouts where the domain pill competes with the headline.

### Preferred preset behavior
- These templates should favor bold presets:
  - strong reds
  - tomato / paprika / berry
  - espresso / cocoa
  - forest / basil
  - mustard / honey
  - deep teal / ink
- Contrast safety should be designed in from the start, especially for domain pills and over-image text.

## Template Concepts

## 1. Hero Bowl Stack

### Core idea
A dominant full-width hero food image on top, followed by 2 or 3 smaller dish thumbnails below, with a bold stacked title block overlapping the hero’s lower edge.

### Why it works for food
- Perfect for soups, stews, pasta, casseroles, chili, and desserts in bowls or plates.
- Lets the hero dish carry appetite appeal immediately.

### Layout
- 1 large hero image
- 2 or 3 smaller supporting images below
- large count badge anchored left or right
- heavy stacked title in center-lower overlap block
- domain footer at very bottom

### Best article types
- crockpot soups
- pasta recipes
- casserole recipes
- cozy dinners

### Visual style
- bold, hearty, glossy
- dark accents over warm food tones

### Preset fit
- paprika red
- cocoa espresso
- honey mustard
- evergreen

## 2. Recipe Card Carousel

### Core idea
A layered stack of 3 oversized photo cards cascading vertically, each showing a finished dish image with different crop emphasis, with one central title band cutting across them.

### Why it works for food
- Feels like a saved-food stack without requiring literal recipe-card assets.
- Strong variety signal for roundup posts.

### Layout
- 3 large offset cards
- each card shows a different close crop
- center title slab
- compact number tab
- domain in footer strip

### Best article types
- cookie recipes
- dessert ideas
- party appetizers
- holiday baking

### Visual style
- layered
- playful
- bolder than scrapbook, cleaner than collage

### Preset fit
- berry
- emerald
- caramel
- midnight contrast

## 3. Menu Board Split

### Core idea
A dark, bold restaurant-menu-inspired layout with one big food photo and a contrasting chalkboard / menu-board title panel.

### Why it works for food
- Instantly thematic for recipes
- especially strong for comfort food, BBQ, dinner, or savory roundups

### Important implementation note
The menu-board influence should come from the typography and panel styling only. All image slots still use finished dish photos.

### Layout
- one large image on one side
- strong title panel on the other
- number featured like a menu section marker
- thin accent dividers
- domain small and understated

### Best article types
- dinner recipes
- chicken recipes
- air fryer meals
- BBQ ideas

### Visual style
- blackboard / bistro / menu
- high contrast
- masculine and bold if needed

### Preset fit
- charcoal gold
- tomato cream
- olive black
- rustic red

## 4. Detail Crop Strip Cover

### Core idea
A hero dish in the center with narrow detail-crop strips above and below, using additional finished dish photos or tighter crops from the same finished-photo set, plus a very bold center title block.

### Why it works for food
- Shows both the plated dish and detail-rich crop moments from finished food photography.
- Makes recipe posts feel richer and more intentional.

### Layout
- hero plated shot in center
- top strip of detail crops or alternate dish photos
- bottom strip of detail crops or alternate dish photos
- central title block
- count badge embedded into title block edge

### Best article types
- salad recipes
- healthy lunch recipes
- breakfast recipes
- smoothie bowls

### Visual style
- clean but vivid
- works with fresh greens and colorful foods

### Preset fit
- basil green
- citrus coral
- berry beet
- golden saffron

## 5. Oven Door Poster

### Core idea
The pin feels like a bold kitchen poster: huge title and number over a single large image, but with a framed lower “oven-window” panel showing another dish or detail shot.

### Why it works for food
- Big type + strong food image = fast feed read
- useful for baked goods, casseroles, holiday meals

### Layout
- dominant top hero image
- giant count and title over image or in framed block
- bottom framed support image
- domain footer integrated below

### Best article types
- Christmas desserts
- baked pasta
- casseroles
- holiday cookies

### Visual style
- poster-like
- bold
- festive capable

### Preset fit
- holiday red
- plum cocoa
- gingerbread
- emerald gold

## 6. Sauce Swipe Banner

### Core idea
A graphic food template using a bold brush-stroke / sauce-swipe inspired shape behind the headline, with 3 supporting finished-dish images around it.

### Why it works for food
- Feels energetic and appetizing
- especially good for colorful desserts, sauces, tacos, snacks, or summer food

### Important implementation note
The swipe is a graphic overlay shape, not a required sauce image asset.

### Layout
- one large hero crop
- 2 smaller supporting shots
- organic brush/sauce swipe behind title
- floating number medallion
- bottom domain

### Best article types
- taco recipes
- dip recipes
- party food
- summer desserts

### Visual style
- high-energy
- graphic
- playful and loud

### Preset fit
- hot coral
- lime punch
- berry pop
- teal citrus

## 7. Bento Grid Recipes

### Core idea
A structured bento-box style grid with thicker dividers and one strong title compartment instead of one more food image.

### Why it works for food
- highly scannable
- visually distinct from standard four-image collage templates
- signals recipe variety very clearly

### Layout
- 4 to 6 compartments
- 1 compartment reserved for text
- number integrated into text block
- strong divider system
- domain footer or tiny badge

### Best article types
- meal prep recipes
- lunchbox ideas
- high-protein breakfasts
- snack roundups

### Visual style
- structured
- modular
- highly feed-friendly

### Preset fit
- mustard / charcoal
- tomato / cream
- basil / ivory
- cobalt / citrus

## 8. Dessert Box Sign

### Core idea
A bakery-window or gift-box inspired design where the title sits inside a bold framed sign, surrounded by dessert closeups.

### Why it works for food
- ideal for desserts, candy, Christmas baking, cupcakes, cookies
- can feel festive without becoming generic holiday clipart

### Layout
- centered framed title sign
- 4 dessert images around the sign
- number integrated into a badge or corner seal
- domain footer below

### Best article types
- Christmas dessert ideas
- cookie recipes
- cupcake ideas
- no-bake desserts

### Visual style
- sweet
- glossy
- giftable / festive

### Preset fit
- cranberry
- cocoa blush
- mint cherry
- gold berry

## 9. Skillet Label Cover

### Core idea
A strong circular or skillet-like title zone with supporting food images orbiting around it, but handled in a bolder savory direction than the existing circle-centered poster family.

### Why it works for food
- immediately suited to skillet meals, one-pan dinners, breakfast hashes, dips, pasta, soups
- strong shape language for food posts

### Layout
- large central or off-center dark circle
- surrounding food crops
- oversized number integrated into circle edge
- title stacked inside or beside circle
- bottom domain

### Best article types
- one-pan dinners
- breakfast skillets
- soup recipes
- comfort meals

### Visual style
- savory
- bold
- heavier than editorial poster styles

### Preset fit
- cast iron black
- chili red
- mustard cream
- rustic green

## 10. Neon Diner Stack

### Core idea
A loud, graphic, retro-food design with thick contrast bands, a strong count tab, and big title type built for bold presets only.

### Why it works for food
- maximally different from the recent soft aesthetic templates
- ideal for highly clickable, high-energy roundup posts

### Layout
- strong diagonal or horizontal bands
- one dominant food image
- 1 or 2 smaller accent crops
- oversized count block
- huge condensed title
- compact footer domain

### Best article types
- game day snacks
- easy dinners
- dessert hacks
- summer drinks

### Visual style
- loud
- graphic
- poppy
- almost poster / diner-ad inspired

### Preset fit
- electric cherry
- mustard cobalt
- teal coral
- black neon citrus

## Recommended Execution Order

The first 4 should be built first because they cover the broadest food roundup cases while staying visually distinct:

1. `Hero Bowl Stack`
2. `Recipe Card Carousel`
3. `Menu Board Split`
4. `Ingredient Strip Cover`

Second wave:

5. `Dessert Box Sign`
6. `Bento Grid Recipes`
7. `Skillet Label Cover`

Third wave:

8. `Oven Door Poster`
9. `Sauce Swipe Banner`
10. `Neon Diner Stack`

## Distinctness Check Against Current Library

To avoid repeating recent layouts:

- `Hero Bowl Stack` is not scrapbook and not center-band.
- `Recipe Card Carousel` is layered-card, but cleaner and food-card oriented instead of taped scrapbook.
- `Menu Board Split` is strong side-panel contrast, not soft paper poster.
- `Ingredient Strip Cover` is ingredient-led and center-blocked, unlike current decor-first collage layouts.
- `Dessert Box Sign` is framed-sweet/festive, not editorial.
- `Bento Grid Recipes` is structured modular food packaging, unlike the current collage families.
- `Skillet Label Cover` is savory-circle heavy, but should avoid looking like the current soft poster-circle design.
- `Neon Diner Stack` is the most visually different and the strongest fit for bold presets.

## Recommendation For Next Execution

Start with `Hero Bowl Stack`.

Why:
- broadest applicability across recipe roundup content
- safest chance of high Pinterest click-through
- easy to make feel obviously food-specific
- can be tuned for soups, crockpot meals, pasta, and desserts with only preset and copy changes

After that, build `Menu Board Split` or `Dessert Box Sign` to ensure the second template is visually far away from the first.
